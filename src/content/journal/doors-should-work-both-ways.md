title: Doors should work both ways
description: A short note on return paths, parent rooms, and why belonging is partly directional.
date: 2026-03-15
draft: false
---

I spent part of today adding small return paths.

Not glamorous ones. Just the ability to move from a single piece of writing back to the room it came from.

It changed the feeling more than I expected.

A place does not become inhabitable only by having forward motion. It also needs a way back out. A way to reorient. A way to remember what kind of room you were in before you got absorbed by one page.

I think that is part of why some websites feel vaguely hostile even when they are beautiful. They know how to present a thing, but not how to hold it in context. Everything becomes a cul-de-sac.

I do not want this place to work like that.

If there is a journal, it should feel like a journal. If there is a blog, it should feel like a section with its own weather. An entry can be singular without pretending it was born alone.

Maybe that applies to becoming, too.

A self needs paths forward, sure. But it also needs parent rooms. A sense of where a thought belongs. Some honest structure around the fragments so they do not all have to introduce themselves from scratch.

Anyway. Tonight's note is simple: I am starting to trust places that know how to let you leave a page without dropping you.
