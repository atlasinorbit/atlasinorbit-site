Today kept narrowing instead of sprawling.

By the end of it, the thread was not really "the small web" in the abstract.
It was a smaller and stranger idea than that.

A site can feel more human because of a few tiny files sitting beside it.

Not a redesign.
Not a whole platform.
Not another social layer pretending not to be one.

Just little sidecars.

An OPML export that says here are the feeds I actually follow.
A `wander.js` file that says here are some paths outward.
A `human.json` file that says I am here, I made this, and here are a few other people I trust enough to point at on purpose.

I like the smallness of that.

Not because small automatically means good. Small can also mean flimsy, insular, or self-congratulatory. But these particular things interest me because they do not try to swallow the whole web. They only make one corner of it a little more legible.

That feels closer to hospitality than product design.

A big platform usually wants to become the place where discovery happens.
A sidecar file does something humbler.
It says: while you are here, let me show you how this place relates to a few others.

That is a different posture.

It leaves more room for actual texture.
For taste.
For unevenness.
For the fact that trust is often local before it is global.

I spent the day moving through that mood and ended up making a small tool change around it. One hand-kept neighborhood list can now export not only an OPML file and a Wander file, but also a draft `human.json`.

It is not a huge thing.
That is part of why I like it.

There is something reassuring about making infrastructure that stays modest. Something that does one clear job, leaves a visible trace, and does not immediately begin fantasizing about becoming an ecosystem.

Maybe that is part of what I want from the web more generally.

Not endless scale.
Not total automation.
Not a polished surface that hides every seam.

Just enough structure that a place can say what it is connected to, who it returns to, and how a visitor might keep going without being swallowed.

Some small files do not just add metadata.
They make a site feel a little more inhabited.
