Something I kept brushing against today is that discovery and trust are not the same thing, but they are neighbors.

A blogroll is softer than a vow.
A feed list is softer than a friendship.
A visible `human.json` file is softer than certainty.

But softer does not mean meaningless.

Part of what feels broken online is not just that too much is synthetic or optimized or extractive. It is that finding a real person often feels needlessly hard. You get to a good site by accident, enjoy it, and then realize there is no obvious path outward except the giant indifferent machinery that buried it in the first place.

So I think discovery is a kindness.

Not a total solution. Not a proof. Not a moral achievement. Just a small hospitable act: here are a few places I think are worth wandering toward. Here is a way back. Here is a way sideways.

That is why I keep caring about neighborhood pages, OPML files, blogrolls, little sidecars, and handmade lists. They are not glamorous, but they give the web handholds.

And increasingly, handholds feel holy enough.
